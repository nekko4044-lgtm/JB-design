## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: The site does NOT immediately read as AI-generated — the full-bleed photography, the dark About section contrast, and the bilingual execution are genuinely distinct. However, there are three structural AI grammar patterns embedded in the scaffold that a designer would notice:

1. **Uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section heading** — This is the absolute ban from the skill guide and the brand register. Hero, Projects, About, Services, Process, Contact — every single section opens with `text-xs tracking-[0.25em] uppercase text-greige` above the h2. The exact same structure, the exact same weight, the exact same color. One named kicker used intentionally is voice; six identical eyebrows is AI grammar.

2. **Glassmorphism as a default system** — Services cards, Process cards, and the Contact form all use `backdrop-blur(24px) saturate(1.8) rgba(248,247,245,0.18)` identically. The absolute ban says "rare and purposeful, or nothing." More critically: these panels sit on top of the `--canvas` background (`#F8F7F5`), which is nearly the same color as the glass fill. The blur is blurring a nearly identical surface — it produces zero visual effect while adding GPU overhead. It's decoration without function.

3. **Numbered section markers (01/02/03) in Services** — Process steps earn their numbers (genuine sequence). The Service categories (`01` Turnkey Design, `02` Furnishing, `03` Express) are not a sequential flow — they're parallel options. The `0{i+1}` is category decoration, which falls into the banned pattern.

## Overall Impression

The photography is doing the heavy lifting and it's working — the hero with Julia's real project images and the dark About section are the two moments that feel most like a real designer made this. The structural problem is the **section grammar is completely flat**: identical eyebrow + two-line h2 + RevealLine appears six times in a row. Visitors scroll from Projects to About to Services to Process without any shift in rhythm or visual temperature. The single biggest opportunity is **breaking section monotony** — not by adding complexity, but by committing to different visual languages for different sections.

## What's Working

**1. Hero photography and transition architecture** — Full-bleed real photos with the masked fade into the Projects section below is technically elegant and visually distinctive. The layered gradient overlay tuned per section is craft.

**2. Dark About section** — Using near-black (`rgba(28,25,23,0.97)`) for the About section creates a genuine temperature break in the page. Julia's portrait with the offset border detail works. This is the one section that doesn't feel like the others.

**3. Mobile responsive strategy** — Desktop grid → mobile Embla carousel for Projects, desktop grid → mobile accordion for Services. Both choices are appropriate for their content type. The carousel showing partial cards signals swipability correctly.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Eyebrow above every section — absolute ban

**What**: Every section uses `text-xs tracking-[0.25em] uppercase text-greige mb-4` as a section label: "PROJECTS / DESIGN PORTFOLIO", "ABOUT", "OUR SERVICES", "PROCESS", "GET IN TOUCH". In Hero it appears again as the `eyebrow` translation key. Six identical eyebrows across six sections.

**Why it matters**: This is the most reliable "AI generated this" signal in 2026. A design director reviewing portfolios would immediately clock it. More practically: when everything is labeled with the same visual treatment, nothing is labeled — the eyebrows become noise the eye skips.

**Fix**: Kill the eyebrow as a system. Keep at most one — the one that carries the most brand voice (probably the hero label or the contact section). For the others, let the section heading and visual context do the work. The h2 copy is strong enough to stand without a preamble.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable typeset`

---

### [P1] Glassmorphism as default treatment

**What**: `backdrop-filter: blur(24px) saturate(1.8)` + `background: rgba(248,247,245,0.18)` is used on three separate components: Services desktop cards, Process step cards, and the Contact form panel. Identical values, identical purpose (just "looks like glass").

**Why it matters**: The absolute ban is explicit. More concretely: these panels sit on `--canvas` (`#F8F7F5`) background — the blur is rendering nothing visually, it's just consuming GPU. The Services and Process grids look like frosted glass panels floating over white, which reads as template decoration, not intentional design.

**Fix**: Remove the `backdrop-filter` and `rgba` fill from Services and Process. Use simple `background: var(--canvas-alt)` or `background: var(--canvas)` with a clear `border-border`. Let the grid structure (the bordered grid lines) provide the visual containment. The Contact form is the one place glass could work — keep it there if it reads well over the background texture.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable polish`

---

### [P1] Syne + DM Sans — both in the reflex-reject list

**What**: `--font-display` resolves to Syne; `--font-body` resolves to DM Sans. Both appear explicitly in the brand register's reflex-reject list as training-data defaults.

**Why it matters**: For a site that explicitly lists "Типовой AI-лендинг" as an anti-reference, shipping the two most commonly AI-generated font choices undermines the entire brief. These fonts aren't wrong — they're recognizable, which is the problem.

**Fix**: This is the identity-preservation edge case — the fonts are already committed and visible in production. But if the site is not yet launched (domain still pending), this is the time to swap. Syne can stay if it's been intentionally chosen as a brand anchor; the key question is whether it was chosen by voice ("this sounds like Julia") or by reflex ("this looks like a modern landing page"). If reflex, swap to something less saturated. The RU locale already uses IgraSans for display — that's a stronger, more distinctive choice.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable typeset`

---

### [P2] Section rhythm is flat — same spacing, same scale everywhere

**What**: Every content section uses `py-28 lg:py-40` padding and `clamp(2rem, 5vw, 3.5rem)` heading scale. Nothing breathes larger or tighter.

**Why it matters**: Pacing is part of the brand. "Elegance · Speed · Result" implies urgency and decisiveness — a page that plods at a uniform pace doesn't feel that way. The Process section in particular should feel quick and confident, not the same weight as the About section.

**Fix**: Create 3 distinct spacing registers: tight (py-16/20 for secondary sections), standard (py-28/40 for current), generous (py-32/56 for the hero-adjacent sections). Vary the heading scale — some sections can use a tighter `clamp(1.5rem, 3.5vw, 2.5rem)` for supporting content, reserving the larger scale for hero-tier moments.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable layout`

---

### [P2] WhatsApp buried — primary conversion path not surfaced

**What**: WhatsApp is listed as a social icon at the bottom of the Contact section. For the target audience (Russian expats + UAE property investors), WhatsApp is the expected and preferred contact channel — not a web form with email.

**Why it matters**: A mobile user on the site for 30 seconds won't scroll all the way to contact. The form requires name + email + message (3 fields). WhatsApp is one tap. Burying the higher-conversion path below the lower-conversion one costs leads.

**Fix**: Add a WhatsApp CTA button to the hero (replacing or alongside "Get a Consultation"). Add it to the header "Get in touch" action on mobile. Or add a persistent floating WhatsApp button on mobile screens. The form stays for visitors who prefer it; WhatsApp becomes visible earlier for those who don't.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable shape`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer)** — coming from Google, first visit, mid-relocation search
- The hero has two CTAs at equal visual weight (brand fill vs glass). Neither one feels definitively "primary." Jordan will hesitate: view projects first, or jump to consultation? A clearer primary/secondary hierarchy would help.
- Section eyebrows ("OUR SERVICES", "ABOUT") feel institutional — they push toward agency language rather than the personal, authored voice the brand principles call for. Jordan reads "OUR" and pictures a team, not Julia.
- Contact form success state shows the CompassStar icon with heading + text — Jordan doesn't immediately see "form submitted." A checkmark or explicit confirmation text is clearer.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)** — on phone, one-handed, between tasks in Dubai
- Primary conversion path (WhatsApp) is at the very bottom of a long page. Casey won't reach it in a 30-second visit.
- Mobile carousel arrows (`w-10 h-10` = 40px) are one pixel below the 44px minimum touch target recommendation.
- Language toggle on mobile is inside the hamburger menu — extra tap for Russian-speaking visitors arriving from a Russian-language Google search result.

**Валентина (Portfolio target persona)** — Russian expat, just bought apartment in Dubai Marina, comparing 3 designers on her phone
- She wants to see: speed, price range, examples that look like her apartment. The "7-14 days" differentiator is buried in the hero description as `text-base` text. It should be a structural feature, not a sentence.
- No price indication anywhere on the landing page. The `/price` link is in the nav but easy to miss. For someone comparing options, the absence of pricing signals is a friction point — she'll leave to check a designer who shows ranges.
- The projects section shows work in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and Dubai Marina — locations relevant to her. But the project cards only show title + location on hover/below image with no visible duration or category at a glance on mobile. Speed claims need visible reinforcement.

## Minor Observations

- `z-[99999]` on header and `zIndex: 999999` on mobile overlay are arbitrary values. A semantic scale (`10 → 20 → 30 → 40 → 50`) would be cleaner.
- The About section `strengths` grid uses `rgba(255,255,255,0.55)` for description text on near-black background. Need to verify this passes 4.5:1 — it's approximately `#888` on `#1C1917` which is borderline (~3.6:1). Fails for body text.
- `placeholder:text-greige/50` on form inputs: `--greige` (`#7C7A76`) at 50% opacity on `--canvas` (`#F8F7F5`) is approximately `rgba(124,122,118,0.5)` → effective value ~`#B9B8B6`. Contrast against `#F8F7F5` ≈ 1.5:1. Fails WCAG 4.5:1 for placeholder text.
- The `<h3>` in the "Other Projects" section of project pages uses `var(--font-syne)` directly instead of `var(--font-display)` — this means the RU locale IgraSans font doesn't apply to these headings.
- Mobile menu overlay uses `style={{ zIndex: 999999 }}` inline instead of Tailwind — inconsistent with how the rest of the z-index is managed.
- No `text-wrap: balance` on any h1-h3. Low effort, high impact for heading copy that might wrap on intermediate viewports.
