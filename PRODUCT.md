# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two equal audiences, both based in Dubai and Abu Dhabi:

1. **Expats relocating to the UAE** — Russian-speaking and English-speaking professionals and families moving to Dubai/Abu Dhabi. They need a trustworthy designer who can furnish and style a home fast, without them being on-site for every decision. They open the site on their phone, often mid-move or mid-search.

2. **Property investors** — Buying apartments in Dubai (Palm Hills, Sobha Hartland, Dubai Marina, Saadiyat Island) to rent out. They want fast turnaround, premium result, and ROI focus. They compare several designers and make decisions quickly.

Both audiences look for evidence of taste, reliability, and speed — in that order.

## Product Purpose

Julia Busigina's portfolio site converts qualified leads into consultation requests. It exists to answer three questions visitors have in the first 10 seconds: "Does she do work I'd want in my home?", "Can she actually deliver?", and "How do I reach her?" The site is also discoverable via Google for "interior designer Dubai" and "дизайнер интерьера Дубай".

Success = a visitor submitting the contact form or tapping the Telegram/WhatsApp link.

## Brand Personality

**Элегантность · Скорость · Результат** (Elegance · Speed · Result)

- Calm, edited authority — not aspirational lifestyle fantasy
- Warm but precise — personal without being casual
- Delivers, doesn't just aestheticize — the 7-14 day timeline is a brand differentiator

## Anti-references

- **Typical AI landing page**: cream background, icon + heading + text card grids, uppercase eyebrows on every section, generic "professional"
- **UAE real estate portals** (Bayut, Dubizzle, Property Finder): glossy, corporate, cold — no personality
- **Instagram interior designers**: rose-gold, emoji-heavy, lots of mood but no process or credibility signals
- **Generic luxury agency sites**: all mood, no specificity — could be any city, any designer

## Design Principles

1. **The work is the argument.** Project photos carry more persuasive weight than any headline. Give them space; let them breathe.
2. **Speed is a feature, not a footnote.** 7-14 days is the competitive differentiator. Reinforce it structurally, not just textually.
3. **Quiet authority.** No shouting, no overselling. A designer who is confident in their work doesn't need to convince you with volume.
4. **One person, not an agency.** The voice is personal and authored. Julia's name and face should feel present, not abstracted behind corporate language.
5. **Bilingual without compromise.** EN and RU are equal citizens — not a translated afterthought. Both audiences deserve full presence.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Minimum WCAG AA contrast on all body and UI text
- Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Bilingual: EN primary, RU complete translation with full parity
